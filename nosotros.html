We built this because we ate alone too.

Vovu is a campus social app built on one simple idea: plans should find you.

The problem

65% of college students report feeling lonely. Not because their campus is empty — it isn't. There are thousands of people within walking distance who want to grab coffee, go to the gym, or just sit in the dining hall with someone.

The problem isn't opportunity. It's the asking. Texting someone "want to hang out?" carries real social risk. What if they're busy? What if they say no? What if it's weird? So most people don't ask. They eat alone. Again.

There's also been a 40% drop in dining hall time since 2019. Students are retreating to their rooms, eating alone, staying on their phones. Not because they want to — but because asking someone to join you feels like too much.

Vovu removes the ask. You post what you want to do. Someone applies, anonymously. You see their vibe — not their name. You both say yes. Then, and only then, names appear. Zero rejection. Ever.

How Vovu works

1
Post a plan. What do you want to do, and roughly when? Your exact location stays hidden.
2
Someone applies anonymously. You see their vibe — activity tags and two bars. No name. No photo.
3
You both say yes. First name, campus email, exact spot revealed simultaneously. Last name never shared.

Our values

Anonymous until mutual yes.

No one sees your name until both parties explicitly agree. No exceptions.

No last names. Ever.

This is a product promise and a legal commitment. We never share last names.

Real plans. Real places. Real people.

No swiping. No matching for its own sake. Every interaction on Vovu is about doing something together — not just chatting indefinitely.

Where we're going

Vovu starts at one campus. Grows to the next. Campus by campus, word of mouth only. No ad spend. No forced growth. Just real students on real campuses who found it useful and told their friends.

.edu verified. That's not just a security feature — it's a commitment to keeping Vovu a space where everyone around you is a real student at your real school.
